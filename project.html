Selected Projects

  • NYC Town and Gown, Climate Vulnerability, Impacts, and Adaptation Analysis: In this project, we are developing a model to generate high-resolution projections (100m) of population by age and race for granular climate change impacts assessment in New York City. We first use multi-regional demographic projection models to generate scenario-based population projections for each of the five counties of NYC. We then downscale these population projections to 100m resolution grid cells within each county. We also use time series analysis to estimate age and race proportions of each grid cell. This represents a significant step to model local-scale spatial heterogeneities in population distribution to identify hotspots of climate risk and vulnerable populations at the city scale.

  • A global projection of population by degree of urbanization: In this project, we are developing a model to generate global projections of the Degree of Urbanization at 1KM resolution. Our proposed model operates at three scales. The first level incorporates variations in the child-woman ratio at the first administrative area level to differentiate subnational geographies based on their prospects in population change. The second level examines the neighborhood effects on the development potentials of a focal cell. Finally, the third level investigates the cell-specific factors. We implement this multi-scale model for both population and built-up land projections and combine the results to uncover the degree of urbanization of each grid cell. (Ongoing Github repository).

  • High-resolution spatio-temporal forecasting of COVID-19 incidence using Machine Learning In this collaborative project with the University of Colorado Boulder, we developed weekly forecasts of COVID-19 incidence rates using Machine/Deep learning models (LSTM, random forest, XGBoost) at the U.S. county level. In addition to using time series of incidence rates, we modeled spatial connectivity between counties using SafeGraph daily mobility data and Meta's Social Connected Index to explore both temporal and spatial associations in forecasting incidence rates. Our model continuously ranked among the top five models submitted to the US COVID19 Forecast Hub that the CDC was using to develop policies. The models and results are included in Paper 1, and Paper 2, and Github repository. .

  • Power Systems Cybersecurity (2016-2021): Proposed two false Data Tolerance Mechanisms for Distributed Mode Estimation: Standard Alternating Direction of Multipliers Method (S-ADMM) and Distributed Alternating Direction of Multipliers Method (D-ADMM) have been proposed to detect oscillations in distributed power systems. Unfortunately, these two methods are vulnerable to false data injection attacks. In this project, we proposed two resiliency mechanisms for S-ADMM and D-ADMM published in IEEE SmartGridComm and ICSS, IEEE Transaction on Smart Grid. This project was funded by the Cyber Resilient Energy Delivery Consortium (CREDC) and the National Science Foundation (NSF).

  • Complex Networks Analysis (2010-2013): (i) Sampling from Complex Networks using a novel link-tracing sampling algorithm, based on the concepts from PageRank vectors, to sample from networks with high community structures (Chaos Paper), (ii) Local Community Detection in Complex Networks using a novel local community detection algorithm based on the random walk probability distribution (Report), and (iii) Social networks topology inference using diffusion information (Manuscript).
