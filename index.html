About Me

I conduct research on the intersections of population, environment, and climate change and am a lifelong enthusiast of geospatial data science, predictive modeling, and machine/deep learning models. At the Population Council, I co-lead a demographic modeling project for human and environmental research and help spearhead the Community Demographic Model (CDM), a set of tools that provide computer simulations of population dynamics and change. CDM has been widely adopted to investigate the relationships between population, economic, climate, and environmental changes. I also carry out research, supported by the European Commission, to showcase the intricate details of population distribution and its significant applications in understanding global urbanization trends and the dynamics between humans and the environment. I explore machine/deep learning models to gain insights into how urbanization and population patterns can be predicted for the future, and how they interact with climate change and environmental hazards. I have also contributed to the COVID-19 forecast hub of the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, collaborating with the University of Colorado Boulder on high-resolution spatiotemporal forecasting of COVID-19 incidence using machine/deep learning approaches.
